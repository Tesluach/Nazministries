Nazarene Youth International (NYI)

Nazarene Youth International is a youth organization that has partnered with the Church of the Nazarene since its inception as the Nazarene Young Peoples Society (NYPS) in 1923. In 1976 it adopted its current name, and focused on young people aged 12 to 23 (later 12 to 29). The South Sudan church of the Nazarene in Uganda is focusing to mobilize the youths to involve in active ministry to reach their generation and to be the future leadership.

Sunday School and Discipleship Ministries International (SDMI)

Sunday School and Discipleship Ministries International (SDMI) reported an average global Sunday School weekly attendance of 703,344 and the Global Discipleship Group attendance was 191,912, for a total of 895,256 (an increase of 52,132 from 2009). The Total Global Responsibility List was 1,690,255 in 2009. In 2016, the Global Discipleship attendance was 1,245,818; a decadal growth rate of 55%. The total global responsibility lists was 1,845,786.

The South Sudan church of the Nazarene is focused to make Sunday school and discipleship ministry to increase the awareness in attendance, small group discussion and spiritual growth for church ministry.

Nazarene Missions International (NMI)

Nazarene Mission International is "the church-relations heart of World Mission within each local church", and "the local-church-based global mobilization and promotional arm of the Church of the Nazarene".

The South Sudan church of the Nazarene focus will be to involves in the Nazarene global outreach in prayer, educating, descipling and giving resource.

Nazarene Compassionate Ministries (NCM)

The Church of the Nazarene compassionate ministries have been instrumental in assisting people in every part of the globe who have been affected by war, famine, hurricane, flood, and other natural and human-made disasters.

The South Sudan church of the Nazarene is holistic ministry in compassionate with compassionate hearts, education and evangelism with whatever God is giving us we are going to shares with the needy: (Matt 25:40)
